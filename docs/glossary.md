# Glossary

**Version:** 1.0  
**Published:** July 2026

This glossary provides the official terminology used throughout The Cralgo Framework.

The definitions contained in this document are considered the canonical reference for all documentation, publications, partnerships, proposals, assessments and implementations based on The Cralgo Framework.

---

# Cralgo Framework

The Cralgo Framework is Cralgo's proprietary organisational intelligence framework that provides a structured approach for discovering intelligence, organising knowledge, enabling execution and continuously improving measurable business outcomes.

---

# Organisational Intelligence

The ability of an organisation to continuously discover, connect, interpret, govern and apply intelligence across people, technology, processes, data and ecosystems to improve decision-making and measurable outcomes.

---

# Intelligence

Structured knowledge that has been interpreted, connected and contextualised sufficiently to support decisions or actions.

---

# Intelligence Capital

The accumulated value of organisational knowledge, relationships, experience, evidence, processes and learning that contributes to long-term organisational capability.

---

# ICM (Intelligence Capital Model)

The conceptual model that describes how intelligence is created, organised, enriched and accumulated within an organisation.

---

# ICP (Intelligence Capital Partnership)

The engagement model through which organisations discover, assess and develop their intelligence capital using The Cralgo Framework.

---

# EIP (Execution Intelligence Partnership)

The engagement model that converts structured organisational intelligence into coordinated execution and measurable business outcomes.

---

# CRIS (Cralgo Intelligence Engine)

The intelligence engine within The Cralgo Framework responsible for capturing observations, structuring knowledge, identifying relationships, generating insights and continuously improving organisational intelligence.

---

# REO

Reflection → Execution → Outcomes.

The operating philosophy of The Cralgo Framework.

REO establishes a continuous learning cycle in which reflections inform execution, execution produces measurable outcomes and outcomes generate new reflections.

---

# Reflection

The structured process of analysing observations, experiences, evidence and outcomes to generate organisational learning.

---

# Execution

The coordinated implementation of intelligence through people, technology, processes and governance.

---

# Outcome

A measurable organisational result produced through execution.

Outcomes may include operational improvements, strategic progress, customer value, financial performance or organisational learning.

---

# Knowledge Graph

A structured representation of relationships between entities, concepts, decisions, observations, systems and outcomes.

Knowledge Graphs enable connected organisational intelligence.

---

# Evidence Repository

The structured collection of observations, research, implementations, decisions, outcomes and supporting evidence generated throughout organisational activities.

---

# Reflection Engine

The capability responsible for transforming observations and evidence into structured organisational learning.

---

# Intelligence Index

A measurable indicator used to evaluate a specific aspect of organisational intelligence.

Multiple Intelligence Indexes collectively contribute to broader organisational assessment.

---

# III (Intelligence Infrastructure Index)

The composite intelligence score produced through assessment of an organisation's intelligence infrastructure.

The III provides a measurable representation of organisational intelligence capability.

---

# IIIM (Intelligence Infrastructure Maturity Model)

The assessment methodology used to evaluate the maturity of an organisation's intelligence infrastructure.

IIIM generates the Intelligence Infrastructure Index (III).

---

# POD Framework

The execution structure responsible for delivering focused implementation through specialised multidisciplinary teams.

---

# CoE Framework (Centre of Excellence)

The governance and enablement structure responsible for maintaining standards, quality, knowledge sharing and continuous improvement across implementations.

---

# Enablement Layer

The organisational capabilities that support adoption, governance, collaboration and operational effectiveness throughout implementation.

---

# Agent Framework

The architectural layer responsible for coordinating autonomous or semi-autonomous software agents operating within The Cralgo Framework.

---

# Outcome Engine

The capability responsible for measuring execution performance, validating outcomes and feeding measurable results back into the Reflection process.

---

# Industry Intelligence Model

A domain-specific implementation of The Cralgo Framework adapted to the unique characteristics of a particular industry or sector.

---

# Commercial Playbook

A structured methodology describing how organisations engage with The Cralgo Framework from discovery through measurable outcomes.

---

# Engagement Model

The defined approach governing collaboration between Cralgo and participating organisations throughout adoption and implementation.

---

# Ecosystem

The network of organisations, institutions and stakeholders that collectively contribute to organisational intelligence.

Examples include:

- Government
- Universities
- Enterprises
- Technology Partners
- Investors
- Industry Bodies
- Innovation Networks
